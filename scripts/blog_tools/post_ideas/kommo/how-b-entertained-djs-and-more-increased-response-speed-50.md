Background
The challenge
In search of a CRM for Event Planners
Customizable. Works well for small business. Scalable as they grow, but not complicated or filled with unnecessary features. Integrates well with existing tools. Provides notifications & reminders. Allows the team to automate work processes. Easily accessible remotely with mobile capabilities. All activity and communications are logged in one central place. Accurate records.
How B_Entertained increased their response speed with a CRM for event planners
Step 1. Defining the Communication Strategy
Be succinct. Always have a call to action. Be present where the customer wants. No cold calling — calls are made only after an initial connection elsewhere. Add emojis and images to the templates. Professional-casual tone.
Step 2. Defining Pipelines
-
The test pipeline is used for developing and testing automations and other features before going live. -
The marketing pipeline focuses on leads from FB Lead Ads, Messenger, and Instagram Direct. -
The website pipeline focuses on leads created from their web form and engagement button. -
The direct pipeline focuses on leads from phone calls and text messages via RingCentral. -
The outreach pipeline focuses on leads created from outreach projects and target market lists, such as schools in a specific district. -
The expo/bridal show pipeline focuses on leads from booth sign-ups and expo registration lists. -
The referrals/networking pipeline focuses on leads from networking events and groups and those referred by clients and partners. -
The 6 paid lead gen pipelines each focus on a particular outside source where leads can find B_Entertained, including Bark, Eventective, Bash, WeddingWire, Thumbtack, and The Knot.
