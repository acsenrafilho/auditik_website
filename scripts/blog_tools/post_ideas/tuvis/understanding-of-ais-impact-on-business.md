Artificial Intelligence (AI) is no longer science fiction and is becoming a reality in day-to-day business. 80% of companies in Latin America are already using AI and plan to allocate more than 15% of their budgets to it in the next few years. These numbers are part of the study “Artificial Intelligence in Latin America 2023: Exploring AI as a driver of transformation in Latin America’s digital frontier,” conducted by NTT DATA in partnership with MIT Technology Review.
In the digital world, cybersecurity has become a daily concern for businesses. Cyber-attacks are on the rise, putting confidential data, systems and corporate reputations at risk. But how can artificial intelligence help with cybersecurity?
Artificial intelligence combined with cybersecurity
Artificial intelligence can analyze large amounts of data from multiple sources to identify patterns of activity within an organization. It monitors aspects such as connection times and locations, traffic volumes, devices and cloud applications used by employees. Having established what is considered normal behavior within the organization’s day-to-day operations, artificial intelligence is able to identify behaviors that are outside the norm and need to be investigated.
AI uses machine learning algorithms to continuously learn from the data it evaluates. When it identifies known cyber threats, it can help contextualize the analysis of those threats, making it easier to understand and prevent fraud.
Artificial intelligence has great potential to support security in organizations. Businesses that invest in technology solutions will benefit from increased efficiency and productivity, better threat detection, faster incident response and reduced costs. As technology advances, there are new opportunities for AI to contribute to more productive organizational environments. However, its implementation in security requires a robust infrastructure, investment and technical expertise. Despite the benefits, the adoption of AI solutions requires caution.
How to identify AI data risks?
The lack of qualified professionals and the high cost of the technology can be initial barriers to this matter, especially as it is a new technology. It is essential that companies understand the risks and implement appropriate safeguards when using AI on a daily basis.
Understanding the risks associated with the use of artificial intelligence is essential to avoid negative consequences. To build a robust defense against these risks, it is important to take preventive measures. Data management must be established, with clear policies for collection, storage and use, to ensure compliance with laws and regulations.
Strong cyber security measures are needed to protect data from unauthorized access and cyber attacks. It is also important to use AI systems that are transparent and explainable, so that the reasoning behind decisions can be understood.
