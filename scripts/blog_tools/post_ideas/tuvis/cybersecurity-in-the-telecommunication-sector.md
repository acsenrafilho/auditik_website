The digital and interconnected world has affected many sectors that have begun to use technology for their operations. In this scenario, the telecommunication industry stands out due to its high dependence on technological systems, which makes it constantly evolve, but also increases the need to expand cybersecurity measures.
According to surveys by Lyncas, among the challenges faced by the telecommunication industry, digital security failures are considered risk factors for the operation of services. These failures not only compromise an organization’s infrastructure, but also expose sensitive data and threaten business continuity. The lack of adequate monitoring and cybersecurity measures leaves organizations more vulnerable to inappropriate business practices, such as unprepared and superficial services, which jeopardize the customer experience. In some cases, these vulnerabilities can lead to penalties for the consumers themselves, such as unauthorized charges, which can affect the company’s credibility and expansion.
Cyber security vulnerabilities
In this technological scenario, industries are facing an exponential increase in cyber threats, which are becoming more complex and sophisticated as the population and organizations become increasingly more digital. Before taking preventative measures, it is necessary to understand the challenges faced by the telecommunication sector.
In the first half of 2024, the telecom industry registered an alarming 177% increase in ransomware attacks, a type of malicious software used to block a victim’s access to their own data. Cybercriminals can then demand a ransom to unlock the system.
These attacks can have a variety of motives, such as financial embezzlement or the leakage of a company’s customer data. In addition, given the importance of the telecommunication sector to the flow of information, a lack of cyber security can result in the unavailability of essential industries, such as health and emergency services, which rely on telecommunications to transmit information and provide support to the public.
These vulnerabilities make the telecom sector an attractive target for cybercriminals. They can exploit weaknesses in servers, extract confidential information and navigate freely through the company’s IT infrastructure, threatening the credibility and operations of organizations. As a result, robust protection against cyber breaches and preventative cyber security solutions are fundamental to the integrity and continuity of operations in the industry.
Cyber security in the telecoms industry
With rapid digital transformation, the urgency of implementing effective cybersecurity measures is a concern for 95 per cent of telecom companies, according to the State of Public Cloud Security report.
As a result, organizations need to develop effective strategies that cover all pillars of the business: educating employees on security best practices, optimizing operations without increasing cyber vulnerability, and creating a contingency plan in the event of a data breach.
