Integrated and Strategic Corporate Communication with Tuvis
Effective communication is one of the fundamental pillars of business performance and competitiveness. In 2025, instant messaging apps have definitively moved beyond being simple tools for quick exchanges and have taken on a strategic role in work organization, team collaboration, and relationships with customers and partners. These platforms increasingly concentrate features that directly impact productivity, decision making speed, and service quality.
Instant messaging services have evolved into complete real time communication environments, integrating text, voice and video calls, file sharing, document collaboration, and process automation. While email remains a relevant channel for formal communication, messaging solutions offer a more agile and less bureaucratic dynamic, well suited to the demands of remote and hybrid work, which are now widely established.
In the corporate context, these platforms play complementary roles. Internally, they facilitate team alignment, project tracking, and the quick resolution of operational demands. Externally, they have become essential channels for customer service, support, sales, and relationship management, especially when integrated with apps widely used by the public, such as WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, and Signal.
Given the wide range of solutions available on the market, choosing a messaging app for corporate use requires evaluating criteria such as stability, information security, integration capabilities with other systems, scalability, and ease of adoption by users. Features like multi device synchronization, conversation history, organization by teams or topics, as well as robust encryption and compliance with data protection laws, are now essential requirements.
Among the most widely used messaging apps by businesses in 2025 are solutions focused on both internal communication and customer engagement. WhatsApp Business maintains its relevance due to its broad reach and user familiarity, making it especially effective for customer service and external communication. Slack has established itself as a benchmark for internal communication with strong integration into corporate tools, while Microsoft Teams stands out in organizations that already use the Microsoft 365 ecosystem. Google Chat, Telegram, and Signal also play important roles, each addressing different needs related to simplicity, automation, or privacy.
However, as companies begin to operate simultaneously across multiple messaging channels, a recurring challenge emerges: communication fragmentation. Managing conversations scattered across different apps compromises visibility into interaction history, makes proper workload distribution difficult, and reduces operational efficiency. In this scenario, channel centralization becomes a strategic factor.
This is where Tuvis stands out as a solution capable of integrating multiple messaging apps into a single management environment. By centralizing channels such as WhatsApp, Telegram, and Signal, the platform allows companies to organize service workflows, distribute conversations among teams, maintain complete interaction histories, and automate processes, all in a secure way and in compliance with the LGPD. This omnichannel approach ensures greater control, standardization, and intelligence across customer and partner communications.
Beyond customer service, the integration provided by Tuvis expands the use of instant messaging across areas such as sales, marketing, operations, and internal support. Communication stops being purely reactive and starts generating strategic data, performance indicators, and opportunities for continuous improvement.
When choosing a messaging solution for a business, it is essential to evaluate not only the app itself but also its ability to integrate channels, ensure information security, and support future growth. In an increasingly connected corporate environment, investing in an integrated communication strategy is critical to achieving efficiency, customer proximity, and operational sustainability.
In 2025, effective business communication means delivering fast, contextualized, and secure responses without losing strategic visibility. Tuvis makes this possible by transforming multiple messaging apps into a unified, organized, and results driven communication experience.
