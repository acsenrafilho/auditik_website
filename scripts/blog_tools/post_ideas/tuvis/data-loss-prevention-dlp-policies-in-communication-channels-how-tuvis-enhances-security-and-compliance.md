What is a Data Loss Prevention Policy?
A DLP policy is a set of rules and controls designed to prevent confidential information from being shared or stored incorrectly, whether due to human error, negligence, or malicious intent.
In today’s context, where sales, support, and service teams communicate with customers through fast channels such as WhatsApp, Microsoft Teams, and integrated CRMs, the risk is much higher. What was once confined to internal servers now circulates across mobile devices, third-party apps, and external clouds.
Why does your organization need a DLP policy?
Without a clear policy, a single mistake can result in:
• Regulatory fines (LGPD, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
• Loss of customer and partner trust after a data leak
• Legal risks involving contracts, intellectual property, or personal data
• Operational disruption caused by system blocks or external sanctions
• Internal disorganization when employees do not know how to handle sensitive data
Key components of an effective DLP policy
• Objective and Scope: Define which types of data need protection and in which channels (WhatsApp, email, CRM, etc.)
• Data Classification: Differentiate between public, internal, confidential, and restricted content
• Roles and Responsibilities: Clarify who approves, implements, monitors, and responds to incidents
• Technical Controls and Monitoring: Encryption, message monitoring, audit logs, and automated alerts
• Incident Response: Containment, investigation, notification, and remediation processes
• Training and Awareness: Ensure every employee knows how to handle sensitive information
• Continuous Review: Update rules and controls annually or after relevant incidents
