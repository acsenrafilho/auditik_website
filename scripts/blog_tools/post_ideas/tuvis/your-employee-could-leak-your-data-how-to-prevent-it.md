We live in an era where data is the most valuable asset of a company. Information about customers, negotiations, trade secrets, and even internal documents has immeasurable value, and precisely because of this, it is increasingly targeted by cybercriminals. But there is one detail that is often overlooked: the threat does not always come from the outside.
Your own employee could be the entry point for a data leak.
How Do Data Leaks Happen Inside Companies?
Data leaks do not only come from external attacks. Many times, they result from internal practices that go unnoticed. Some examples include:
- Improper file sharing in personal messaging apps.
- Corporate emails sent to the wrong contacts, exposing sensitive information.
- Use of unauthorized platforms to manage customer information.
- Lack of access control, allowing strategic documents to circulate without monitoring.
These situations may be accidental, but they can also indicate bad intentions. In either case, the result is the same: risk of fines, loss of reputation, and financial damage.
What Are the Risks for the Company?
- Heavy fines for non-compliance with LGPD or other data protection laws.
- Exposure of strategies and trade secrets.
- Financial fraud and scams using customer data.
- Damage to brand reputation, with loss of market trust.
How to Prevent Employees from Leaking Data
Prevention begins with a strong information security culture. This includes regular training, clear policies on system usage, and strict access controls.
But to take it a step further, it is essential to rely on technology that helps monitor, log, and protect all interactions carried out in day-to-day corporate operations.
